About Me

Hardware First.
Build. Test. Iterate.

I'm Abdulaziz AlHumaid, a fourth-year Electrical Engineering student at UBC Okanagan, graduating in 2026. My work is rooted in hardware design and physical implementation — I'm most productive when I'm building something real. Code and simulation are tools I use to serve the hardware, not the end goal.

My projects span RF and wireless communications, VLSI chip design in 130 nm CMOS, analog biomedical sensing, power electronics, and electromechanical motor systems. Each project has sharpened a different dimension of my engineering practice.

My capstone project — a High-Altitude Platform communications payload — validated long-range wireless links over 5.4 km in Kelowna using LoRa, Wi-Fi HaLow, and LTE on a self-contained platform weighing 591 g, built for under $375 CAD.

I care about designs that are fully understood, well-documented, and reliable enough to hand off to the next engineer with confidence.
