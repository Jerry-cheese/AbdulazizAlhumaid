About Me

Engineer. Builder.
Problem Solver.

I'm a fourth-year Electrical Engineering student at UBC Okanagan, graduating in 2025. My work sits at the intersection of hardware and software — from designing analog front-ends at the transistor level to writing firmware that brings physical systems to life.

My engineering journey has taken me through RF communications, VLSI chip design, power electronics, motor control, and biomedical sensing. I'm drawn to projects where the constraints are real and the stakes are meaningful.

My capstone project — a High-Altitude Platform communications payload — validated long-range wireless links over 5.4 km in Kelowna, BC, using LoRa, Wi-Fi HaLow, and LTE on a self-contained, battery-powered platform weighing under 600 g.

Outside the lab, I care about building things that last: clean designs, well-documented code, and systems that can be handed off and understood by the next engineer.
