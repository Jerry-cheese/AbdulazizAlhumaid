About Me

Hardware Designer.
Builder. Problem Solver.

I'm a fourth-year Electrical Engineering student at UBC Okanagan, graduating in 2026. My work is firmly rooted in hardware — I'm most at home when I'm designing a circuit, winding a coil, or debugging a board on a bench. The code I write exists to bring hardware to life, not the other way around.

My experience spans RF communications, VLSI chip design, power electronics, motor systems, and biomedical sensing. Each project has sharpened a different dimension of my engineering instinct — from transistor-level layout in a 130 nm process to tuning an HF antenna simulation to achieve the right beam direction.

My capstone project — a High-Altitude Platform communications payload — validated long-range wireless links over 5.4 km in Kelowna using LoRa, Wi-Fi HaLow, and LTE on a self-contained platform weighing 591 g and built for under $375 CAD.

I care about building things that are honest: designs that are fully understood, documented clearly, and reliable enough to hand off to the next engineer with confidence.
